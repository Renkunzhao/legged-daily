# Legged Daily - 2026-05-18

## Summary
- ETH / RSL shows the strongest current cluster: a new whole-body Unitree G1 locomotion paper plus the public GrandTour dataset and starter tooling.
- GrandTour looks worth tracking as a durable infrastructure asset for legged perception, SLAM, and state estimation work.
- KAIST's DreamWaQ++ remains a strong quadruped perception-control signal, especially for multimodal RL over stairs, slopes, moving platforms, and sensor failures.
- Two recent review papers are useful orientation anchors: one on perceptive legged locomotion in the real world and one on legged robotics in non-inertial environments.
- Current company-side hiring signals remain strongest around NVIDIA GEAR and Amazon robotics teams.

<details>
<summary><strong>New Papers</strong></summary>

- [Learning Whole-Body Humanoid Locomotion via Motion Generation and Motion Tracking](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17335)
  Source: arXiv
  Importance: high
  Summary: Terrain-aware whole-body humanoid locomotion for Unitree G1 that combines diffusion-based motion generation with RL motion tracking and reports hardware results on boxes, hurdles, stairs, and mixed terrain.

- [GrandTour: A Legged Robotics Dataset in the Wild for Multi-Modal Perception and State Estimation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18164)
  Source: arXiv
  Importance: high
  Summary: Large open-access legged-robotics dataset spanning diverse indoor and outdoor environments with multimodal sensing and high-precision ground truth for perception, SLAM, and state estimation.

- [Evaluation of an Actuated Spine in Agile Quadruped Locomotion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07988)
  Source: arXiv
  Importance: medium
  Summary: Empirical study showing that an actuated spine can improve simulated quadruped agility across running, stair climbing, steep slopes, hurdling, and crawling.

- [A Survey of Legged Robotics in Non-Inertial Environments: Past, Present, and Future](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20990)
  Source: arXiv
  Importance: medium
  Summary: Useful review of modeling, state estimation, and control for legged robots operating on moving, tilting, or accelerating support surfaces.

- [Learning Perceptive Legged Robot Locomotion in the Real World: A Systematic Review](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11313692)
  Source: IEEE Robotics & Automation Magazine
  Importance: medium
  Summary: Recent review focused on learning-based perceptive locomotion, organized around application capabilities and perception-integration methods.

</details>
